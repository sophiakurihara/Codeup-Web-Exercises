How old is the National Park Service?
The National Park Service was created by an act signed by President Woodrow Wilson on August 25, 1916. Yellowstone National Park was established by an Act signed by President Ulysses S. Grant on March 1, 1872, as the nation's first national park.
How many areas are in the National Park System?
The system includes 417 areas covering more than 84 million acres in every state, the District of Columbia, American Samoa, Guam, Puerto Rico, and the Virgin Islands.

What is the origin of the National Park Service arrowhead?
The arrowhead was authorized as the official National Park Service emblem by the Secretary of the Interior on July 20, 1951.

Yellowstone National Park

  • Yellowstone National Park is named after the Yellowstone River, the major river running through the park.
  • Yellowstone is in the top five national parks for number of recreational visitors.
  • Swimming is not recommended, and is occasionally prohibited, because most lakes and streams are dangerously cold.
  • Eagle Peak in the southeastern part of Yellowstone is the highest at 11,358 feet.

Yosemite National Park

  • First protected in 1864, Yosemite National Park is best known for its waterfalls.
  • The Tioga Road (Highway 120 through the park, from Crane Flat to Tioga Pass) is open approximately late May through October, though these dates vary depending on conditions.
  • There are three giant sequoia groves in Yosemite National Park: the Mariposa Grove, the Tuolumne Grove, and the Merced Grove.
  • Animals in the park are wild, but many are accustomed to people because they have been fed so often. It is illegal to feed any animal in the park.

Zion National Park

  • Bicycles are allowed on the 7-mile Zion Canyon Scenic Drive. Due to safety concerns cyclists are required to pull over and stop when approached from behind by a shuttle bus, and NEVER PASS A SHUTTLE BUS.
  • Zion Lodge operates a restaurant year round and a snack bar from April through October.
  • Closest camping is available in Watchman and South campgrounds, located near the south entrance.
  • Rockfalls are very common in Zion.
